**Nudge** is a free, open-source Android app blocker built over a weekend out of two dislikes: paying for apps that should be free, and letting trillion-dollar companies have unfettered access to a fragmenting attention span. It's especially good against infinite short-form video — Reels, Shorts, TikTok — and made with ADHD in mind.

Its signature move isn't a hard lock — it's **friction**. Before a blocked app opens, you wait through a short breathing exercise or countdown. That's enough to break the subconscious autopilot loop and put *intention* back at the front of your mind.

## Features
- ⏳ **Delay-to-open** – A breathing exercise or countdown before a blocked app opens — the feature no other open-source blocker has
- 🎯 **In-app feature blocking** – Kill YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels/Explore, or the TikTok For You feed *without* blocking the whole app
- 🚫 **Hard blocks, time budgets & app groups** – Full control per app or per group
- 📅 **Schedule rules** – Block social media 9–5 on weekdays; supports overnight schedules
- 🔢 **Interaction counter & auto-kick** – Floating overlay counts reels scrolled; auto-sends you home after N
- ⚫ **Grayscale mode** – Make apps less appealing on demand
- 🔒 **Zero internet permission** – Declared in the manifest. Not a promise — a guarantee. All data local (Room DB), no analytics, no telemetry, GPL-3.0.

Open source, always free, and you can read every line of the code that has access to your screen.
